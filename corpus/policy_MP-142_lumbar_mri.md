# Meridian Health Plans — Medical Policy MP-142

**Magnetic Resonance Imaging of the Lumbar Spine**

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the medical necessity criteria used by Meridian Health Plans ("the Plan") when reviewing prior authorization requests and claims for magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) of the lumbar spine, including CPT codes 72148 (MRI lumbar spine without contrast), 72149 (with contrast), and 72158 (without and with contrast). It is intended to promote the appropriate use of advanced imaging consistent with published clinical guidelines while avoiding exposure of members to unnecessary diagnostic procedures.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all outpatient, non-emergent requests for lumbar spine MRI submitted on behalf of Plan members aged 18 and older. It does not apply to imaging performed in the emergency department, imaging for members with known or suspected malignancy with new neurological findings, or imaging required for surgical planning where surgery has already been authorized. Requests falling outside this scope are reviewed under the Plan's general utilization management procedures.

## 3. Definitions

**Conservative therapy** means non-surgical, non-imaging management of low back and radicular symptoms, directed by a licensed provider. Qualifying components include supervised physical therapy, prescription or provider-directed nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs), and structured activity modification.

**Provider-directed** means ordered, prescribed, or formally recommended by the treating provider and reflected in the medical record; self-directed exercise alone does not qualify.

**Objective neurological deficit** means a reproducible abnormality on physical examination, such as a positive straight-leg raise, a diminished deep tendon reflex, dermatomal sensory loss, or focal motor weakness.

## 4. Medical Necessity Criteria

Lumbar spine MRI is considered medically necessary when **all** of the following criteria are met and documented in the medical record submitted with the request.

### 4.1 Conservative Therapy Duration

The member has completed at least **six (6) weeks** of provider-directed conservative therapy (physical therapy, NSAIDs, and/or activity modification) within the six (6) months immediately preceding the request. The medical record must document the start and end dates of the conservative therapy course. Undated references to "a course of therapy" do not satisfy this criterion.

### 4.2 Persistent Symptoms or Objective Deficit

The member has persistent symptoms despite the conservative therapy described in §4.1, **or** the record documents an objective neurological deficit on examination (e.g., positive straight-leg raise, diminished deep tendon reflex, dermatomal sensory loss, focal motor weakness).

### 4.3 No Recent Prior Lumbar MRI

The member has **not** undergone lumbar spine MRI within the twelve (12) months preceding the request, unless the record documents a new or progressive neurological deficit arising since the prior study. Imaging history must be verifiable from the submitted record.

### 4.4 Code Consistency

The requested CPT code is consistent with the documented ICD-10 diagnosis. Requests in which the procedure code does not correspond to the diagnosis of record are returned for coding review.

## 5. Non-Covered Indications

Lumbar MRI is not considered medically necessary for uncomplicated acute low back pain of less than six weeks' duration without neurological findings, for routine surveillance of stable chronic degenerative changes, or when requested solely at member preference in the absence of the criteria in §4.

## 6. Documentation Requirements

The submitting provider must include: the ordering provider's clinical notes; documentation of conservative therapy with dates (therapy records, medication history, or equivalent); the neurological examination findings relied upon; and the member's lumbar imaging history for the preceding twelve (12) months.

## 7. Appeals

A member, or a provider acting on the member's behalf with the member's written consent, may appeal an adverse determination issued under this policy. A **first-level appeal must be filed within one hundred eighty (180) days of the date printed on the denial notice.** The appeal must identify the specific criteria in §4 being disputed and must attach the supporting documentation the appellant believes satisfies those criteria. Appeals are reviewed by a licensed clinician not involved in the original determination. The Plan issues first-level appeal decisions within thirty (30) days of receipt for pre-service requests.

## 8. References

Plan medical policy is informed by generally accepted clinical guidelines for the management of low back pain and lumbar radiculopathy. This policy is reviewed annually by the Meridian Medical Policy Committee.
